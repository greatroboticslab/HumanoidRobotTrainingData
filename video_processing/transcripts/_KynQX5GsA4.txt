 Rice has been cultivated for over 10,000 years, originating in the Yangtze River Valley of China. By 2000 BC, rice spread to Southeast Asia and India, becoming a staple in diets and agriculture. There are over 40,000 varieties of rice, with major types including itica, japonica, and glutinous rice. Rice plays a vital role in cultures worldwide. Featuring in dishes like rosado, biryani, and sushi. Today, rice is grown on every continent except Antarctica. With China and India being the largest producers from ancient fields to modern kitchens, rice continues to be a cornerstone of global food security.
