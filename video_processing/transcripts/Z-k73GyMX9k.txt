Sriracha peppers are relatively easy to grow, providing you give them the right conditions. Like most peppers, they thrive in warm, sunny environments. If you're starting from seed, plant the seeds indoors about 8 to 10 weeks before the last frost date. The seeds need a warm environment, ideally between 70 and 80 degrees Fahrenheit, to germinate. Tomatoes are another great companion, as they share similar growing conditions, and planting them together can optimize space in the garden. Avoid planting sriracha peppers in their fennel, as they compete for nutrients and potentially stunt growth. One interesting behavior of the sriracha pepper plants is their ability to thrive in containers, making them perfect for patio or balcony gardens.
