 Ancient Mesopotamia, often called the cradle of civilization, showcased remarkable vegetable gardening practices. The fertile land between the Tigris and Euphrates rivers allowed for advanced agriculture. Farmers utilized irrigation systems, diverting river water to their fields, enabling year-round cultivation. They grew a variety of vegetables, including onions, garlic, and lentils. Crop rotation was practiced to maintain soil fertility, alongside the use of organic fertilizers like animal manure. Ancient Mesopotamians also implemented tools like sickles and plows made from wood and metal. Their deep understanding of seasonal cycles and plant needs laid the groundwork for modern agricultural techniques, showcasing their innovative spirit.
